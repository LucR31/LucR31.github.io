Positive Hopf plumbed links
This is the topic of my doctoral thesis, University of Bern, Bern, 2017-2021. Thesis supervised by S. Baader. I studied links constructed with positive Hopf links, using an operation called plumbing. And how, when they satisfy given conditions, these are equivalent to one of the links denoted by \(A_{n}\), \(D_{n}\), and \(E_{6}\), \(E_{7}\), \(E_{8}\). As well as to what extent that equivalence holds.
Generating synthetic data using AI
Capstone project of the data science Bootcamp, Zurich, 2022. The project was proposed by the SIX group. Join work with D. Bomberger, K. Krylowicz and N. Bidaux. We generated synthetic data using two well-known algorithms in the field of deep learning: GAN and Variational Autoencoders.
Gravitational lensing and galaxy clusters
Research collaboration with the Astrophysics Department of the University of Oviedo, 2020-2021. Remote. Join work with Cueli M. M., Gonzalez-Nuevo J., Bonavera L., Crespo D., Casas J. M., Lapi A. We explored the magnification bias provoked by roughly three thousand galaxy clusters from the SSDS (Sloan Digital Sky Survey). The background sources, nearly seventy thousand, were taken from the Herschel space observatory.
Decomposition of the Kronecker product of two Jordan matrices
Undergraduate research, Wayne State University, Detroit, 2013-2014. Project supervised by A. Salch. We showed that the Kronecker product \(J_{2}(\lambda) \otimes J_{n}(\mu)\) is congruent to \(J_{n-1}(\lambda\mu)\oplus J_{n+1}(\lambda\mu)\) for characteristic different from two, and \(J_{n}(\lambda\mu)\oplus J_{n}(\lambda\mu)\) for characteristic equal to two.
Self-charging robot
This is an independent project. I started it during my bachelor's degree in Detroit. Just for fun. The goal is to create a robot whose only purpose is to seek light for self-charging. Using an Arduino Uno board, a motor shield controlling two motors, two light and touch sensors, a solar panel, and 5x1.25V rechargeable batteries.

ABOUT 

Photo taken by baixaulifoto.com

I was born in Valencia, Spain, in 1990. Since I was a little kid, I have been passionate about science, technology, and swimming! I was an elite swimmer for five years, hitting the top ten in the Spanish nationals several times and becoming a silver medalist in the 2012 Spanish Swimming Nationals. I received an athletic scholarship to continue my mathematical studies and swimming career in the USA (Detroit city!). Where I successfully manage to combine sports and studies till 2015. Check it out: WSU-athletics

That same year I got accepted to pursue a master's degree in mathematics at the University of Miami, so I got a tiny Chev car and drove from Detroit to Miami, where I made a local friend called Geico (a wild iguana that visited me every morning).

In 2017 I went to Switzerland, and four years later, I graduated with a Doctorate in Mathematics from the University of Bern, specializing in low-dimensional topology and exploring the intricate properties of some of the most exciting mathematical knots and links.

Shortly after that, and given my increasing interest in Machine Learning, I continued my career in the AI field, studying data science at Zurich SIT Academy.

Currently, I am working as a data science teacher at Le Wagon coding academy, in Zurich. I am a member of the Astronomical Valencian Association AVA and enjoy swimming and sailing.
