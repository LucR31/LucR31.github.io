Generating synthetic data using AI

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

Capstone project of the data science Bootcamp, Zurich, 2022. The project was proposed by the SIX group.

Companies nowadays handle large amounts of data and use it for many types of statistical analysis. In most cases, this data contains the personal information of many users. A solution to this problem is to generate synthetic data, i.e., data preserving the statistical properties of the real data, yet fake.

In this project, we generated synthetic data using two well-known algorithms in the field of deep learning: Generative Adversarial Networks and Variational Autoencoders. We also used not-so-known variations of these algorithms. Our conclusions agree with the current research. There is a trade-off between the usability and the privacy of the synthetic data.

Jordan decomposition of the Kronecker product of two Jordan matrices

MATHEMATICS

Undergraduate research, Wayne State University, Detroit, 2013-2014.

A Jordan block is a square matrix of size \(n\) with \(\lambda\) in the diagonal, ones in the superdiagonal, and zeroes elsewhere, denoted by \(J_{n}(\lambda)\). A Jordan matrix is a diagonal matrix, where the diagonal is composed of Jordan blocks, usually denoted as \(J_{n_{1}}(\lambda_{1})\oplus\dots\oplus J_{n_{r}}(\lambda_{r})\).

We showed that \(J_{2}(\lambda) \otimes J_{n}(\mu)\cong J_{n-1}(\lambda\mu)\oplus J_{n+1}(\lambda\mu)\) for characteristic different from two, and \(J_{n}(\lambda\mu)\oplus J_{n}(\lambda\mu)\) for characteristic equal to two. What about \(J_{3}(\lambda)\otimes J_{n}(\mu)\)? And for \(J_{m}(\lambda) \otimes J_{n}(\mu)\)?
