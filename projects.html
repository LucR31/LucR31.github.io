Positive Hopf plumbed links
This is the topic of my doctoral thesis, University of Bern, Bern, 2017-2021. Thesis supervised by S. Baader. I studied links constructed with positive Hopf links, using an operation called plumbing. And how, when they satisfy given conditions, these are equivalent to one of the links denoted by \(A_{n}\), \(D_{n}\), and \(E_{6}\), \(E_{7}\), \(E_{8}\). As well as to what extent that equivalence holds.

Gravitational lensing and galaxy clusters
Research collaboration with the Astrophysics Department of the University of Oviedo, 2020-2021. Remote. Join work with Cueli M. M., Gonzalez-Nuevo J., Bonavera L., Crespo D., Casas J. M., Lapi A. We explored the magnification bias provoked by roughly three thousand galaxy clusters from the SSDS (Sloan Digital Sky Survey). The background sources, nearly seventy thousand, were taken from the Herschel space observatory.
Decomposition of the Kronecker product of two Jordan matrices
Undergraduate research, Wayne State University, Detroit, 2013-2014. Project supervised by A. Salch. We showed that the Kronecker product \(J_{2}(\lambda) \otimes J_{n}(\mu)\) is congruent to \(J_{n-1}(\lambda\mu)\oplus J_{n+1}(\lambda\mu)\) for characteristic different from two, and \(J_{n}(\lambda\mu)\oplus J_{n}(\lambda\mu)\) for characteristic equal to two.
Self-charging robot
This is an independent project. I started it during my bachelor's degree in Detroit. Just for fun. The goal is to create a robot whose only purpose is to seek light for self-charging. Using an Arduino Uno board, a motor shield controlling two motors, two light and touch sensors, a solar panel, and 5x1.25V rechargeable batteries.
